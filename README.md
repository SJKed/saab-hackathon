# The Challenge: Build a Prototype of a Command System for the Air Defense of the Future

An air defense system does not only need to detect threats – it also requires fast, intelligent decisions on how to meet each threat. In a world where the situational picture changes from second to second, the ability to optimize resources in real time is a critical component to ensure air defense superiority and the ability to protect the nation's interests.

## Immediate decisions are required

When a threat is detected:
- Which aircraft should be sent from which bases?
- Which effector is the right one — robot, air defense, or drone?
- How is continued capability ensured when resources are depleted and new threats emerge?

The system must not only solve the immediate crisis — it must think several steps ahead.

## The task

Build a prototype of an intelligent decision support system that revolutionizes air defense's ability to achieve tactical superiority through efficient resource allocation and high operational flexibility.

### Alternative task

Build a prototype of a simulation engine to evaluate the above-mentioned decision support system.

# Jury Judging Criteria

How convinced are you that:

- The solution will help users succeed better at something that is very business-critical?
- The solution would significantly improve users' ability to succeed at this?

---

# Decide What to Build & Present

## Key Questions to Answer in Your Demo

**User goals**
- Who are the users?
- What are they trying to achieve?

**Impact**
- How critical is this to the business?
- Why?

**Inevitable activities**
- What steps must the user inevitably take to reach their goal, regardless of process or tool?

**High-impact suboptimalities**
- How does your solution optimize these activities or their coordination?

**Breakthrough solution structure**
- What breakthrough solutions are used?
- How is the solution structured?

**Functionality**
- Demonstrate how the user can perform the inevitable activities significantly better than before.

---

# Limit the Scope

The time at a hackathon is very limited, both for building and presenting.

- Focus on building one of the most interesting parts of a complete solution.
- You will not have time to demonstrate more than that.

Approach:
1. Present the full solution concept briefly.
2. Demonstrate the most interesting and impactful part.
